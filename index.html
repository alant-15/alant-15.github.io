Hello/Buenas! I am a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at Brown University, advised by Dr. Diana Freed. Previously, I graduated from the University of Massachusetts, Amherst in 2024, with a B.S. in Computer Science and a certificate in Criminology and the Criminal Justice System. I'm interested in investigating technosolutions through the lenses of computer science, law, and sociology. Additionally, I'm exploring how societal norms influence the design and production of social technologies and how these impact the flow of personal data and infringe on privacy.

UMass + Massachuetts Attorney General's Office

As an undergraduate at UMass Amherst, I worked as a Digital Foresnics intern under the Massachusetts Attorney General's office, under the guidance of my incredible mentor Laura Hernandez. I worked on extracting, processing, and analyzing digital evidence from criminal cases, I was also incredibly fortunate to work with Dr. Brian Levine on the App Danger Project at the RescueLab in the Cybersecurity Institute.
